## Dish Descriptions

### Tajine
A traditional Moroccan dish slow-cooked in a conical earthenware pot. The unique shape allows steam to circulate, creating tender, flavorful results.

### Couscous
Steamed semolina granules served with vegetables and meat. A staple of North African cuisine, traditionally served on Fridays.

### Pastilla (B'stilla)
A sophisticated pie made with thin phyllo pastry, traditionally filled with pigeon (now often chicken or seafood), almonds, and spices, dusted with cinnamon and sugar.

### Harira
A hearty soup made with tomatoes, lentils, chickpeas, and meat, traditionally eaten to break the fast during Ramadan.

### Msemen
Square-shaped Moroccan pancakes with a flaky, layered texture, often served with honey or jam.

### Zaalouk
A cooked salad made from eggplant and tomatoes, seasoned with garlic, cumin, and paprika.

### Kefta
Ground meat (usually beef or lamb) mixed with spices and herbs, shaped into meatballs or kebabs.

### Merguez
Spicy lamb or beef sausage flavored with harissa, cumin, and other North African spices.

### Briouats
Triangular pastries filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables, similar to samosas.

## Cultural Notes

### Traditional Eating Times
- Breakfast: Msemen, Harira
- Lunch: Couscous (especially Friday), Tajine
- Dinner: Lighter dishes, soups
- Snacks: Briouats, Kebabs

### Serving Suggestions
- Tajines are traditionally eaten with bread
- Couscous is served family-style from a large platter
- Pastilla is often served at celebrations
- Harira is the traditional Ramadan break-fast soup

### Spice Profile
Common spices in Moroccan cuisine:
- Cumin
- Coriander
- Cinnamon
- Paprika
- Saffron
- Ginger
- Turmeric
